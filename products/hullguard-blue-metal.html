Flexible Protection for Marine Metal Surfaces (SPV-224)

Known in the industry as the "Blue Metal Guard," Hullguard Blue Metal+ (SPV-224) is a translucent blue PVC-based surface protection tape widely trusted for protecting stainless steel and aluminium — both in marine environments and across the processed metals industry.

This film is highly conformable, making it ideal for curved or irregular metal components found across a vessel, such as winches, cleats, and structural panels. It's engineered with a special acrylic adhesive designed for outdoor use, delivering strong adhesion while ensuring clean removal with no residue.

Hullguard Blue Metal+ offers excellent UV resistance and weather durability for up to 9 months, making it perfect for dry dock storage or transport in all types of conditions. Fully RoHST2 compliant, this film is both practical and reliable for long-term protection.

Application Information

Proper Application For Best Results

  • 1

    Surface Preparation

    Clean the metal surface thoroughly, removing all contaminants, oils, and moisture. For polished metals, use an appropriate solvent that won't leave residue.

  • 2

    Measuring and Cutting

    Pre-cut pieces to appropriate sizes for complex shapes. For irregular surfaces, cut pieces slightly larger than required to allow for positioning adjustments.

  • 3

    Application Technique

    For curved surfaces, start from the center and work outward, allowing the film to conform naturally to the shape. Apply steady pressure to ensure proper adhesion.

  • 4

    Securing Edges

    Pay special attention to edges, especially on complex shapes. Use a soft cloth or squeegee to firmly press down all edges to create a secure seal.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is the blue color of Hullguard Blue Metal+ visible through the film?

    Yes, Hullguard Blue Metal+ has a translucent blue tint that makes it easily identifiable when applied. This blue coloration helps installers quickly verify coverage areas and identify any spots that may have been missed during application. The tint is specifically designed to allow visibility of the protected surface beneath while still providing a visual indicator of the film's presence.

  • How does Hullguard Blue Metal+ handle exposure to saltwater?

    Hullguard Blue Metal+ is specifically engineered for marine environments and offers excellent resistance to saltwater exposure. The special acrylic adhesive maintains its integrity even when occasionally exposed to salt spray or splash. However, it's not designed for continuous submersion. For best results in areas frequently exposed to saltwater, we recommend periodic inspection of the film edges to ensure continued protection.

  • Can Hullguard Blue Metal+ be applied to anodized aluminum surfaces?

    Yes, Hullguard Blue Metal+ is specifically designed to work well with anodized aluminum surfaces. The adhesive system is formulated to provide strong adhesion to anodized finishes while still allowing clean removal without damage to the anodization layer. This makes it ideal for protecting aluminum components during construction, transportation, or storage.

  • What temperature range can Hullguard Blue Metal+ withstand once applied?

    Once properly applied, Hullguard Blue Metal+ can withstand temperatures ranging from -20°C to +80°C (-4°F to +176°F), making it suitable for use in a wide range of climates and conditions. This broad temperature resistance ensures reliable protection whether the vessel is stored in cold northern marinas or southern boatyards with high sun exposure.
